Forecasting the spread of COVID-19 using deep learning
My undergraduate dissertation project, for BSc Mathematics. This project entailed original research into the deep learning architectures LSTMs (long-short term memory) and GCNs (graph convolutional networks) in time-series forecasting. It won the 'Best Novel Programming Project' Prize in 2025 from the University of York and the Leeds-based medical software company, TPP.

I found that the most effective deep learning models were the LSTMs, however, my work suggests that statistical methods such as ARIMA are still the gold-standard for time-series prediciton.
